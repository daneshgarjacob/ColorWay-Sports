The Dodgers dropped their 2026 World Series Edition uniforms, and this is the third championship collection in six years. That's enough to start comparing them. Here's how all three stack up.

## 2026: The Best One Yet

This year's collection feels like a mix of the last two, and it works. The details are what separate it.

The hat's the best part. Gold brim, two stars on the side for back-to-back. Small detail, but it hits. It doesn't scream "champion" -- it just quietly lets you know.

The jersey is white with a gold Nike swoosh and the championship patch on the sleeve. Nothing overdone, nothing weird. It just looks right.

## 2024: The Gold Standard

<div style="display: flex; justify-content: center; margin: 0 0 2em;">
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-dnt="true"><a href="https://twitter.com/Dodgers/status/1905808040978493852"></a></blockquote>
</div>

Last year's went heavy on the gold. A lot of people had a problem with it. We didn't. It was loud, sure, but it matched the moment. Not every championship collection needs to play it safe.

**Grade: A-**

## 2020: Clean but Basic

<div style="display: flex; justify-content: center; margin: 0 0 2em;">
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-dnt="true"><a href="https://twitter.com/Dodgers/status/1380608664763965445"></a></blockquote>
</div>

The most understated of the three. It was fine. The Roman numeral VII for the franchise's seventh title was a nice touch, but beyond that, there wasn't a lot going on. Compared to what came after, this one feels like a first draft.

**Grade: A-**

## The Verdict

Three titles in six years, three collections, and you can see the progression. 2020 was safe. 2024 was bold. 2026 takes the best parts of both and lands in the middle. That's the sweet spot.
